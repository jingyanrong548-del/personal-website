  • IIR — International Institute of Refrigeration

    Intergovernmental organization (est. 1908) for refrigeration, heat pumps, and the cold chain; FRIDOC database, publications, conferences, and membership.

    Latest publication (Apr 2026)

    The IIR Publishes a Technical Brief on High-Temperature Heat Pumps: A Key Technology for Industrial Decarbonisation

    61st IIR Technical Brief on Refrigeration Technologies · DOI 10.18462/iir.TechBrief.03.2026

    Lead author: Ruzhu Wang (Shanghai Jiao Tong University). Co-authors per IIR FRIDOC: Z. Xu, B. Palm, M. Kim, Y. Dong, J. Feng.

    Main contents

    • HTHPs (heat above 80°C) for industrial decarbonisation: ~37% of global energy use is industrial heating; 100–200°C is a core target band for food, chemical, textile, paper, and plastics processes.
    • Three routes—vapor-compression, absorption, and hybrid absorption–compression—with simultaneous heating/cooling and thermal-storage integration.
    • Working fluids, compressors, commercial products (about 90–300°C, kW to 100 MW), case studies, and COP >1.5 vs boilers; economics tied to electricity–fuel price ratios and grid carbon intensity.
    • Policy levers: performance-based subsidies, demonstrations, carbon pricing, workforce training, and clarity on refrigerant phase-out timelines; includes a two-page Summary for Policymakers.

    Latest market intelligence (2026 editions · 2025 sales year)

    Worldwide Heat Pump Markets 2026 — with World Domestic & Commercial Boilers Market 2026

    BSRIA Market Intelligence · primary research late 2025–Q1 2026 · ~3.8 boilers sold per hydronic heat pump in 2025

    Main findings (global)

    • 2025 sales: ~3.5 million hydronic heat pumps (+4% after a weak 2024); just under 13 million boilers sold (~90% gas). Boiler market broadly flat; transition remains gradual.
    • Ratio 3.8 boilers per hydronic heat pump in 2025; ~200 million boilers in operation worldwide (~half older non-condensing units).
    • ~73% of global heating demand is replacement/retrofit (>90% in mature markets)—the main competitive arena for boilers, heat pumps, hybrids, and district heating.
    • Forecasts: hydronic heat pumps +8% CAGR (2019–2029); fossil fuel boilers −2% CAGR (2019–2029). By 2029, hydronic heat pumps ~12% of replacement/retrofit activity. Progressive 2035 EU scenario: ~+20% heat pump growth vs ~−7% gas boilers in top five European heating markets.
